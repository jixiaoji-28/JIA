04 / Current research

Micro-scale resistance in older adults' walking environments

Master's research Ongoing

Conventional accessibility measures emphasize distance or travel time, but they can overlook street-level conditions that make a short trip difficult for an older pedestrian.

This project develops a micro-scale spatial resistance framework by combining street-view imagery, road networks, terrain data, and spatial indicators. The goal is to identify street segments where environmental conditions may create greater walking difficulty and to support more age-friendly urban design.

Question
How can fine-grained environmental barriers be represented in pedestrian accessibility analysis?
Data
Street-view imagery, road networks, terrain and slope data, and spatial indicators
Methods
GIS analysis, image segmentation, terrain analysis, and spatial index construction
Contribution
A human-centered measure of walking difficulty that complements conventional accessibility metrics
